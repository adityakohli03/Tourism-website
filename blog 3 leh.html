LADAKH

BLOG ON LADAKH

nov 6, 2019

Manali-Leh is this beautiful highway trip that bikers use as a rite of passage. It’s a 470-kilometer journey from one end to the next through different altitudes and beautiful scenery.The valley of Ladakh extends far beyond Leh and even a few months at a stretch might not be enough to explore the entire Ladakh region. Much of Ladakh is accessible only by foot, a large part of it is beyond civilian access and all of it is a high altitude Himalayan region.

Things to Do While You are Here

nov 5, 2019





The public transport buses connecting Ladakh ply up to Leh. Leh is also where the airport is. Being the district headquarters as well as the amalgamation point for all tourists visiting Ladakh, Leh has the maximum tourist facilities, which come close to the material comforts one is used to in cities. While enjoying these comforts, it’s important to keep reminding yourselves that Ladakh is a high altitude cold desert. Natural resources, like water (and air) are hard to come by. Wastage of anything here is a sacrilege.

Medical facilities There is a hospital in Leh which is run by the Indian Army. There’s a separate tourist section in the hospital. The most common complaint among the tourists is related to the altitude. You can buy a small can of oxygen and keep it with you at all times, as you step out of Leh and travel through the rest of Ladakh.

Accommodation The gaining popularity of Ladakh as a tourist destination means the choice of places to stay at in Leh only keeps increasing every year. Rapid mobile network penetration and internet has brought people of this remote region closer to the outside world, giving them a clearer idea of what the tourists need. From luxury hotels to economical guest houses to personalised homestays – there’s ample accommodation in Leh.
