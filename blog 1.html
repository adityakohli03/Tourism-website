AMRITSAR

BLOG ON THE GOLDEN TEMPLE CITY

Title description, Dec 7, 2017

Steeped in history, culture and tradition, Amritsar is the holiest abode for Sikhs across the world and welcomes tourists with open arms. You’re welcomed by the bustling streets, the aroma of food cooking in the open-air kitchens and strains of devotional music the moment you step in the bustling by-lanes.

Things to Do While You are Here

nov 5, 2019





Golden Temple: The Harmandir Sahib, famous in the world as the Golden Temple, is the holiest shrine of the Sikh religion. The dome on the upper section is gilded with about 100 kilograms of pure gold, hence the name. While you’re here, offer prayers inside the temple, where the sacred Guru Granth Sahib is placed, take a dip in the holy sarovar and eat food at the langar.

Jallianwala Bagh: This public garden is the site of the 1919 Amritsar Massacre. There is a well inside where many people including children jumped to save themselves from the firing. You can also see the bullet marks along with the walls. A light and sound show is hosted here every evening.

Wagah Border: It is home to the Radcliffe Line, which is the demarcation between India and Pakistan and was drawn during the partition of India in 1947. The illustrious lowering of the flags ceremony takes place here, where soldiers from both sides try to outdo one another as the crowds cheer on.
